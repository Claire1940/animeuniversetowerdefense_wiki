<Section icon={Swords} title="Solving the Chess Board Game Puzzle">

After speaking to your distressed subject, you must find a board game in a location familiar to them. The map you need to load into depends entirely on the anime origin of your specific **anime universe tower defense distressed subject**.

### Map Selection by Subject Origin
- **Naruto NPC**: Load into **Ninja Forest** (Act 1).
- **One Piece NPC**: Load into **Marine Base** (Act 1).
- **Bleach NPC**: Load into **Hollowed Moon** (Act 1).

Once inside the correct map, search the environment for chess boards. There are usually 3 to 4 boards spawned across the map, but only **one** is correct. Step on a board; if a chess minigame interface does not pop up, move on to find the next board.

<Section icon={Shield} title="Mid-Quest Milestones: Waves, Bosses, and Relics">

The middle portion of the Power of Kings quest line transitions from exploration to gameplay milestones. These steps require optimization to avoid wasting valuable resources like gold and time.

### 1. Complete 80 Waves in a Single Game
This step requires you to reach Wave 80 in a single match. Story mode acts do not have enough waves, making **Infinite Mode** your only option. Bring your strongest DPS units and slow/stun supports to survive the scaling enemy health pools.

### 2. Kill 15 Boss Enemies
Instead of playing long Infinite runs to find bosses, use the fast-restart method:
- Load into the first map's **Infinite Mode**.
- Play until **Wave 10** when the first boss spawns.
- Defeat the boss, immediately exit back to the lobby, and restart.
- This method takes roughly 25 to 30 minutes to complete all 15 kills.

### 3. Upgrade Any Relics 75 Times
The quest tracker counts individual upgrade clicks, not the number of levels gained. If you use the "Max Upgrade" option on a high-tier relic, it will only count as **one single upgrade** toward your quest progress.

| Relic Upgrade Strategy | Gold Cost | Quest Progress Gained | Efficiency Rating |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Max Upgrade Option** | High (5,000+ Gold) | 1 Point | Very Poor |
| **Single Level-One Upgrades** | Low (~1,000 Gold total) | 75 Points | Excellent (Recommended) |

<Callout type="success" title="Gold Saving Trick">
Gather multiple Level 1 common relics. Upgrade each relic one level at a time (Level 1 to Level 2), then switch to the next relic. This keeps your enhancement costs at the bare minimum.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Fastest Way to Complete the 5,000 Placements">

The final quest step before unlocking Lelouch requires you to place any unit 5,000 times in any game mode. Doing this manually is incredibly tedious, but you can automate the process using a macro tool like TinyTask alongside a cheap placement unit like Speedwagon.

| Unit Placement Setup Step | Action Required |
|---|---|
| **Step 1** | Equipping the Unit | Put **Speedwagon** (or your cheapest placement cost unit) in your active loadout. |
| **Step 2** | Entering Match | Start any game mode and intentionally let the base health drop to zero to trigger the defeat screen. |
| **Step 3** | Preparing the Interface | Close the victory/defeat results window by clicking the **X button** (do not click replay). |
| **Step 4** | Recording the Macro | Start recording on TinyTask. Place Speedwagon, immediately sell him, and repeat this loop 5 times. |
| **Step 5** | Loop Execution | Stop recording and set TinyTask to continuous play. Let the macro run until the 5,000 placement goal is met. |

<Callout type="warning" title="Macro Safety">
Ensure your macro recording includes a slight delay between placing and selling to account for any server-side ping fluctuations.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Lelouch (Lulu) Unit Showcase and Meta Analysis">

After completing the 5,000 placements, claim your final reward to unlock **Lelouch (Lulu)**. He is a specialized support unit who does not deal direct attack damage on placement, instead relying on commanding mechanics and crowd control.

### Lelouch Ability Breakdown
- **Geass Passive**: Lulu issues strategic orders instead of attacking. His cooldown determines how quickly he can issue a new command after his previous Geass effect breaks.
- **Stand Still**: Stuns targeted enemies for 4 seconds. While stunned, these units will collide with other incoming enemies, dealing mutual collision damage based on their health pools.
- **Retreat**: Forces enemies to walk backward for 40% of the duration they maintain direct eye contact within Lulu's range.
- **Attack Order**: Forces enemies to walk backward for 2 seconds, triggering collisions with other units along their path.

| Upgrade Level | Cost | Primary Effect | Strategy Utility |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Placement** | Low | Unlocks Geass Passive & Stand Still | Basic Crowd Control |
| **Upgrades 1-6** | Moderate | Significant Cooldown Reduction | Faster Command Rotations |
| **Upgrade 7** | High | Unlocks **Absolute Control** | Applies current order to every enemy on map |
| **Upgrade 10** | Very High | Unlocks **Beam Ability** | Deals 100% of unit damage via stun beam |
