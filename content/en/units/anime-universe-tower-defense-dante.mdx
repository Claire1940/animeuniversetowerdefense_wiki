<Section icon={Swords} title="Upgrade Path & Stats Analysis">

Upgrading Dante during a match is critical to unlocking his true potential. His early levels focus on single-target damage, making him excellent for early-wave defense. However, as the waves progress and enemy density increases, investing in his mid-to-late upgrades becomes necessary to unlock his sweeping area-of-effect capabilities.

The most significant power spike occurs at Level 5, where his attack pattern shifts to a full 360-degree circle slash. This allows him to hit every enemy within his range, drastically increasing his overall damage output. Level 6 introduces his signature Devil Trigger state, which adds a persistent burn effect to all struck targets, melting high-health bosses over time.

<Callout type="tip" title="Cost-Efficiency Strategy">
It is often better to fully upgrade a single Dante to Level 6 rather than placing multiple Level 1 or Level 2 units. The jump in DPS and the addition of the Devil Trigger burn effect provide far more value per gold spent.

When analyzing his stats, Dante's low Seconds Per Attack (SPA) is his greatest asset. At maximum upgrade level, he attacks every 1.5 seconds. When combined with a high base damage of 16,000, his raw DPS exceeds 10,000 before factoring in any external buffs or elemental advantages. This makes him one of the most cost-effective ground units in the game.

<Section icon={Map} title="Placement Strategies & Team Synergy">

Maximizing Dante's damage output requires precise positioning on the map. Due to his circular AoE at higher upgrade levels, placing him at the center of U-turns or loop sections of the track ensures he maintains maximum uptime on passing enemies. Avoid placing him on long, straight paths where his circular range is partially wasted on empty space.

Additionally, Dante shines when paired with support units that can slow down fast-moving targets. This keeps enemies within his attack zone for a longer duration, allowing his rapid attack speed to deal maximum damage.

<Callout type="warning" title="Placement Warning">
Do not place Dante too close to the map entrance. If he targets fast-moving scout units early, his slow-starting attack animations might miss them entirely. Place him slightly further back where enemies begin to group up.
</Callout>

When building a team around Dante, prioritize units that offer attack speed buffs or damage amplification. Support characters who can freeze or stun enemies are also highly recommended. By locking enemies in place, Dante can continuously apply his Devil Trigger burn effect without any downtime.
